About

Research Interests

In my research, I seek to explore how ML can be used to transform the classical EE fields of signal processing, networking, communication systems. I particularly enjoy statistics and algorithmic development. I am currently seeking admittance to MS and PhD programs in the fields of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. After graduate studies, I plan to enter industry working in advanced research and development.

My ongoing bachelor’s thesis is with Prof. Mark Coates on the topic of evaluation methods for categorical generative models, a type of unsupervised machine learning. The research proposes an algorithm to rank the performance of categorical generative models over very large sample spaces (of order 1616) and is based on statistical first principles and hypothesis testing. Planned future work involves applying this research to the task of protein sequence modeling. The results of my work were submitted recently to the IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing (ICASSP 2023) and are under review.
