Research Interests

In my research, I wish to explore how ML can be used to transform the classical EE fields of signal processing, networking, and communication systems. I particularly enjoy statistics and algorithmic development. Next year, I will be attending the Delft University of Technology in the Netherlands. After graduate studies, I plan to enter industry working in advanced research and development.

My bachelor’s thesis recently commenced with Professor Mark Coates on the topic of evaluation methods for categorical generative models, a type of unsupervised machine learning. The research proposes binning, an algorithm to evaluate the performance of categorical generative models over very large sample spaces (of order 109) and is based on statistical identity testing literature. The binning metric provides correct, meaningful, and interpretable results with statistical guarantees which outperform baselines. The binning method is verified on the real world task of generative protein sequence modeling. A paper is published in the IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing (ICASSP 2023). A second paper is under review with Uncertainty in Artificial Intelligence (UAI) 2023.
